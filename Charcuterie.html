Charcuterie

Where did the word 'Charcuterie' come from?

The term 'charcuterie' comes from the French words 'chair' and 'cuite' which loosely translates to "cooked meat."

Types of Charcuterie

  • Serrano Ham:
    Serrano ham is made from special pigs in Spain. It is actually a slightly less expensive version of Jamon Iberico de Bellota which can be cured for years!
  • Salami:
    Salami is one of the most popular types of charcuterie. It is generally sweeter and aged.
  • Bresaola:
    Bresaola is an air dried, salted beef. It is aged and can sometimes have a smoky flavor.
  • Prosciutto:
    Prosciutto is an extremely popular type of charcuterie. Prosciutto is silky and is served best sliced paper thin. In Italy they say you should be able to read a newspaper through it!
  • Speck:
    Speck is very similar to prosciutto but instead of curing it, it is smoked. Often times producers will age the prosciutto and then smoke it to turn it into speck.
  • Mortadella:
    Mortadella is a cooked sausage made from different parts of pork. Some people consider it similar in texture to bologna. Mortadella also occasionally has the signature pistachio nuts throughout the meat.

The Production Process

In America we traditionally go with the quickest and most efficient method available to us. While this can be a great technique for many things, traditional charcuterie and specialty meats is the opposite. Master charcutiers take as long as they feel necessary to achieve the desired flavor. Meats can even be aged for years! The process is fairly simple, but the aging and perfect ratios of spices are what makes these time consuming, high end meats that much more worth it.
Once the Charcutier has mixed in the spice blend(if they need it) with the meat they move on to the next step. Charcuterie usually begins with rubbing salt into the surface of the meat. The salt draws out the water from the meat, resulting in a moisture free product. This is process is known as curing.
From here on out most of the process is entirely based around the aging time and size. One particulary unique and famous technique is used by Columbus Craft Meats in San Francisco. Columbus Craft Meats is located right along the Bay Area so during the foggy mornings they actually open their doors and windows to access the meat and help it age.
